2023 Program

-solo-

Roberto Alagna

Friday june 9th - 9pm

On the international lyrical scene for nearly 40 years, the Franco-Sicilian tenor has added more than 60 roles to his repertoire and successfully makes regular incursions into song. Authentically popular and loved by a wide audience, seduced by all aspects of singing and insatiable passion for the voice, constantly exploring new artistic territories, the artist leads an unprecedented career.

José Carreras

Saturday june 10th - 9pm

José Carreras is one of the most illustrious tenors of his generation. Born in Spain in 1946, José Carreras discovered music as a child. His early and already remarkable performances were followed by a solid training at the Barcelona Conservatory. He performed his first tenor role in an opera in 1970: Flavio, in Bellini's Norma. His musical intelligence, his ability to embody the most diverse roles as well as his sumptuous voice make him a breathtaking tenor, a total artist.

-duo-

Anna Netrebko

Sunday june 11th - 9pm

Throughout an illustrious career beginning in 1994, Anna Netrebko has arguably become a reigning prima donna of the 21st century. Born in 1971 in Krasnodar, Russia, Netrebko studied vocal performance at the St. Petersburg Conservatory and completed the Merola opera program in San Francisco. In live performance and on numerous award-winning recordings, Netrebko's portrayals of opera's most iconic heroines demonstrate remarkable range and artistic sensibility.

Natalie Dessay

Sunday june 11th - 9pm

Internationally known, Natalie Dessay began her career in the coloratura soprano repertoire. The artist expands her repertoire over the years to get closer to Belcantist heroines, while continuing to defend the French repertoire. She is the first French opera singer to have been named Kammersängerin by the Vienna State Opera.
